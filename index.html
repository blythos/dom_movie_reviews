Welcome! Allow me to explain the review system here. You can give a film a rating out of five gold stars, and out of five hearts. The gold stars indicates the classical quality of the film. The hearts represent the sheer entertainment value.

For example, a film like Schindler's List might get five gold stars, but only two hearts because while it is an excellent piece of filmmaking, it is pretty tough going. Contrast it to Tommy Wiseau's The Room, starring Tommy Wiseau, written by Tommy Wiseau, directed by Tommy Wiseau, produced by Tommy Wiseau, and funded by Tommy Wiseau: this would probably only get one gold star, but five hearts because it's a genuinely amazing thing to behold.
